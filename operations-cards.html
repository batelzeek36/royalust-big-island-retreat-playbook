1) Safe Operating Mode

Lowest-Risk Path (Now)

  • Now (lowest risk): run day-use, private educational workshops under PMA.
  • Frame as "educational retreats / sustainability workshops" — fits Ag District uses.
  • Later (if overnights): either partner with permitted lodging OR pursue County permits for accommodations + TAT filings.
2) Structures

Keep Them Legal or Exempt

  • Use only permitted or exempt accessory structures (non-habitable, ≤600 sq ft on Ag).
  • NO permanent sleeping or plumbing in exempt sheds/yurts.
  • File Ag-exempt declaration with County if required; keep acknowledgment in binder.
  • Tents >700 sq ft → Fire Dept. permit.
  • Assembly load ≥300 → Assembly permit.

3) Health, Food & Sanitation

DOH & On-Site Requirements

  • Food prep = DOH Special Event Food Establishment permit OR licensed caterer.
  • Portable toilets + hand-wash stations required; keep service receipts.
  • Document compliance in binder.

Sustainable Sanitation Solution

For remote Big Island locations where traditional septic systems aren't viable, we're implementing the Cinderella Comfort Incineration Toilet.

This electric incineration system eliminates waste completely, requires no water connection or septic system, and produces only sterile ash. Perfect for our sustainable retreat operations in remote agricultural areas.

4) Make the Event Private

Membership-Only Operations

  • Membership application → approval → signed agreement.
  • Website: "Private, members-only; application/invitation only."
  • No public ticket sales.
  • Gate signage: "Private Event — Members & Invited Guests Only."
  • Maintain roster on site.
5) Neighbor & Nuisance Controls

Respect the Village

  • Headcount cap tied to parking capacity.
  • Noise plan & curfew (quiet hours).
  • On-site parking only; use cones/attendant.
  • Neighbor contact phone at entrance; document response.
6) Compliance Binder

Bring to Every Event

Must have at every event:

  • TMK zoning printout + Code Ch.25 excerpts.
  • EPIC/Building docs + ag-exempt declarations.
  • Tent/Fire permits, food permits, portable toilet contract.
  • Insurance certs (general liability + participant accident).
  • Member roster + signed agreements + safety briefing sheet.
  • Incident log.
7) Taxes & Filings

GET / TAT Discipline

  • Collect/remit GET on dues/fees.
  • Overnight consideration = TAT (10.25%) + County 3%.
  • File TA-1/TA-2 + County portal.
  • Keep PMA books separate from LLC.
8) Tripwires (Auto Pause)

Instant Stop Conditions

  • Adding sleeping/plumbing to exempt structure.
  • Using tents >700 sq ft without Fire permit.
  • Advertising public tickets.
  • Scaling headcount beyond safe parking/noise.
  • Any neighbor complaint or inspector visit → pause + remediate.
9) Reassurance to Richard

Why This Is Safe

We're locking this down on four fronts:

  1. Private, invitation-only educational day-use retreats under PMA.
  2. Only permitted or exempt non-habitable Ag structures (with tent/FD permits as needed).

  4. A compliance binder + neighbor-friendly operating plan.
